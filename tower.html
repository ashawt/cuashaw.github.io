Evaluation of the Tower Website

The Tower's website has many pros and cons. In terms of visibility of system status, it is a pro: for example, when I clicked on a sports article, it showed related articles at the bottom of the page. On the main page, the articles and tabs are easy to access, and the website uses images and large text to make it more visible. In terms of User control and Freedom, the user is in control as they can choose which article to go to. If users click the Tower name, they can return to the main menu. The tabs are easy to understand, and the wording is not technical. The main con is the archive. While it is good to make the archive visible, it might be easier to create a tab and section for it, as the section can add more content to the archive. For teachers, the Tower is a good site for international issues and local political events. In terms of recognition rather than recall, the information is easy to find, with large titles and easily digestible content. In terms of flexibility and efficiency of use, the website is easy to navigate and efficient; you can always return to the home page. In terms of aesthetic and minimalistic design, the website is minimalist, with only white and beige used for colors, and most articles use a single image. In terms of the match between the system and the real world, the article's content is always connected to the title. If I were an undergraduate teacher looking for World War 2, I would not recommend this website. The website is easy to navigate, and the articles are accessible; however, the articles are not relevant, and the website's archives do not go back to the 1940s. The closest article about war is by Patrick D Lewis, published on March 21, 2024, on the Ukraine War, whereas the rest is about sports and irrelevant politics.

Top Strengths and Weaknesses

Strengths

  • S1: Easy Navigation-The site is easily navigable due to its organization and search engine. The site is organized into tabs with themes such as politics, news, and sports. The search engine allows you to discover articles quickly, with minimal wait time.
  • S2: Articles are accessible. The articles are accessible, as students do not need a subscription to access them. The articles are also accessible linguistically as the writing is clear and concise

Weaknesses

  • W1:Articles relevance-When I typed World War Two, the results were not relevant. This is a weakness as it means that for history, the Tower cannot be considered a relevant source as their articles do not cover World War Two but only the recent conflicts
  • W2: Archives timeline - When looking at the archives, the oldest year was 2015. This is a weakness because articles are primary sources, and not having access to them would force them to look elsewhere.
